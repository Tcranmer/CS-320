- How can I ensure that my code, program, or software is functional and secure?
    Proper testing and following the guidelines for programming are good starts to ensure functional software. As you design and code a new feature or a core functionality, test it before moving on. This helps ensure that every step of the design process, you know your code works and is secure within your program.

- How do I interpret user needs and incorporate them into a program?
    Like trying to understand your neighbor when they complain that your fence is too long, it starts with proper communication. Keep proper communication of every step in the design process helps ensure user needs are met as the program is made.
    Incorporating begins in designing each piece of the program around the desires the user laid out in the communication, generating functions and classes around the sole premise on what the user and your design lead agreed upon.

- How do I approach designing software?
    My first step is asking myself, what is the sole premise I am programming for. Is it an entity I am making that does different features, an object that interacts with computer features, or a procedurally generated item?
    After I have the premise down, I start designing with my or the client's desires in mind. I create the pieces of the "puzzle" based on what is needed, testing each piece before slotting it into the puzzle. Once I know the piece is good
    I incorporate it and ensure the piece plays well with the full puzzle. When the puzzle is complete, I do 1 last round of testing, clean up the design and add amplifying comments and ship it out.
